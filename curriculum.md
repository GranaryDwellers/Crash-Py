# National Curriculum Links

IT and Computing are taught in middle school to the [National curriculum](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-computing-programmes-of-study/national-curriculum-in-england-computing-programmes-of-study)

There is a distinct difference between IT, and computing.  IT is about using a package made by someone else to achieve a goal. Computing is about using the computer itself to achieve a goal.  IT encompasses tools such as word processors, spreadsheets, presentaion tooling. Computing encompases writing algorithms using data structures to achieve a goal.

IT should be taught in the context of the lessons that directly concern the tool.  English and languages are good lessons to teach word processing, and presentation skills, in the context of communicating. Mathematics is a good vehicle for teaching spreadsheets in the context of modelling a problem. The sciences, including social sciences, are a useful vehicles for all of the tooling as it lies in the intersection, using mathematics, and communications skills together to achieve a goal. Arts particularly music and graphics, have their own sets of tooling that are generally not considered in the traditional realm of IT, but are absolutely relavent in this context.
